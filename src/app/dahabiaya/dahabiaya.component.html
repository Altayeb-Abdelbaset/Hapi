traditional Egyptian riverboat that has been used for centuries, particularly on the Nile River. The name "dahabeya" comes from the Arabic word "dahab," meaning gold, which historically referred to the gilded decoration often found on these boats. Dahabeyas are usually elegant, shallow-bottomed vessels with two sails and were originally used by aristocrats and wealthy travelers during the 19th century to cruise the Nile in comfort and style. They are typically powered by the wind and have large, spacious decks that offer stunning views of the river and surrounding landscapes.

Today, dahabeyas have been revived as luxury cruising boats, providing an intimate and leisurely way to explore the Nile. They often feature modern amenities while retaining their classic charm, offering an alternative to larger cruise ships. A journey on a dahabeya typically includes visits to ancient Egyptian temples, tombs, and other archaeological sites along the river, with the advantage of accessing smaller, less crowdedÂ locations.
